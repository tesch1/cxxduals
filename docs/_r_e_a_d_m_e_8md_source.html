1 ## cxxduals
2 
3 *Under Construction!*
4 
5 Template header library for [dual
6 numbers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dual_number) in C++. Dual
7 numbers are related to complex numbers, but &epsilon; is nilpotent.
8 Nesting provides hyperdual numbers, and hyperhyperdual numbers, etc...
9 
10 Also compiles under CUDA.
11 
12 [![Build Status](https://api.travis-ci.org/tesch1/cxxduals.svg?branch=master)](http://travis-ci.org/tesch1/cxxduals)
13 [![Coverage Status](https://coveralls.io/repos/github/tesch1/cxxduals/badge.svg?branch=master)](https://coveralls.io/github/tesch1/cxxduals?branch=master)
14 
15 Using dual numbers in place of basic types provides a simple way to compute the derivative of a function, because:
16 
17 \f$ f(x + &epsilon; y) = f(x) + &epsilon; f'(x) y \f$
18 
19 Thus, to calculate \f$ f'(3) \f$, set \f$ y = 1 \f$, \f$ z = (3 +
20 &epsilon; 1) \f$ and take the \f$ &epsilon;\f$-part of \f$ f(z) \f$ =>
21 \f$ epart(f(z)) := f'(3) \f$
22 
23 `cxxduals::dual<>`, `cxxduals::dual<float>`
24 
25 ## Usage
26 
27 ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~{.cpp}
28 
29  #include <cxxduals/dual>
30  using namespace cxxduals;
31 
32  ...
33  dual<double> x;
34  x = {3,1};
35 
36  std::cout << "x=" << x << "\n";
37  std::cout << "x*x=" << x*x << "\n";
38  std::cout << "epart(x*x) = d(x*x)/dx = " << epart(x*x) << "\n";
39 
40 ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
41 
42 Before including the header, some "#defines" will modify what's done by
43 the header:
44 
45 ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~{.cpp}
46 
47 #define CXXDUALS_NO_LIMITS // disable specialization of std::numeric_limits<>
48 #define CXXDUALS_NO_TYPEDEFS // disable typedefs for `dualf,duald,dualld,dualcf,dualcd,dualcld`
49 #include <cxxduals/dual>
50 
51 ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
