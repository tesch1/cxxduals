1 ## cxxduals
2 
3 Template header library for [dual
4 numbers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dual_number) in c++. Dual
5 numbers are related to complex numbers, but &epsilon; is nilpotent.
6 Nesting provides hyperdual numbers, and hyperhyperdual numbers, etc...
7 
8 Also compiles under CUDA.
9 
10 [![Build Status](https://api.travis-ci.org/tesch1/cxxduals.svg?branch=master)](http://travis-ci.org/tesch1/cxxduals)
11 
12 Using dual numbers in place of basic types provides a simple way to compute the derivative of a function, because:
13 
14 \f$ f(x + &epsilon; y) = f(x) + &epsilon; f'(x) y \f$
15 
16 Thus, to calculate \f$ f'(3) \f$, set \f$ y = 1 \f$, \f$ z = (3 +
17 &epsilon; 1) \f$ and take the \f$ &epsilon;\f$-part of \f$ f(z) \f$ =>
18 \f$ epart(f(z)) := f'(3) \f$
19 
20 ## Usage
21 
22 ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~{.cpp}
23 
24  #include <cxxduals/dual>
25  using namespace cxxduals;
26 
27  ...
28  dual<double> x;
29  x = {3,1};
30 
31  std::cout << "x=" << x << "\n";
32  std::cout << "x*x=" << x*x << "\n";
33  std::cout << "epart(x*x) = d(x*x)/dx = " << epart(x*x) << "\n";
34 
35 ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
36 
37 Before including the header, some #defines will modify what's done by
38 the header:
39 
40 ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~{.cpp}
41 
42 #define CXXDUALS_NO_LIMITS // disable specialization of std::numeric_limits<>
43 #define CXXDUALS_NO_TYPEDEFS // disable typedefs for `dualf,duald,dualld,dualcf,dualcd,dualcld`
44 
45 ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
